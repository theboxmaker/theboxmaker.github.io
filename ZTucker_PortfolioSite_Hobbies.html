Rock Climbing

  I began rock climbing in the late summer of 2023, when my girlfriend and I tried it out for a date night. WE loved it, and decided to go back every now and then. What started out as a date night turned into quite the hobby for us both. I think the most fun part that dragged us into the sport was how inviting and friendly it is. As far as sports communities go, I think climbing might be the most diverse and supportive. I've been going to our gym for almost 2 years now and I still see so many different people coming in to climb. I've seen people from every age group and every walk of life. The best part about that is how everyone there is supportive of each other. I remember being a beginner climber, struggling to get up the most basic climbs, being cheered on and supported by random strangers who could climb a lot harder than me. It doesn't matter what you're climbing, the people in the climbing community will cheer you on and offer help if you want it.

  The two factors that kept me coming back to climbing are the physical workout and the mental workout as well. Climbing is obviously a very physical sport, so you get a really good workout from doing it. This is especially true if you're just starting out because you'll be using muscles that don't get used elsewhere. For me, it also makes the workout fun. I am not good at going to a traditional gym and hitting a routine every week. That gets boring, but at a climbing gym you're having fun and getting a workout at the same time!

  The mental challenge is also extremely fun to me. The harder the climb, the more precise you have to be with your movement, grip, and technique. There are a variety of ways to climb each climb depending on the individual's strengths and weaknesses. My strength is in my technique, as I'm not as physically strong as the other guys I climb with. To keep up with them, I have to have better technique. It is such a good feeling when you have been struggling with a climb and feel like it is impossible, just to change one thing about the way you've been doing it and then make the entire climb so much easier! There is no better feeling than working hard and finally jumping off the wall having tried your hardest and succeeded.

Golf

  My other hobby is golf. I started playing golf a year before I started rock climbing, and I love it. There's so much to like about it. It is, yet again, a thinking sport. Above every shot you have so much to think about before hitting the ball. You have to consider what type of grass you're hitting off of, is there mud or water on the ball, is the ball above/below/or even with your feet, how far away is the hole, and so many more factors. I enjoy being challenged like in golf. Not only does it make you think, it tests you. I've seen a lot of people I play with get REALLY mad at this game. You can make one bad shot, and if you let it affect you, your entire round could suffer from it. It's a game that requires you to have a good amount of mental fortitude if you want to be able to put up a good number.

  All these challenges given to you by golf, and I haven't even mentioned the swing mechanics. This is another extremely important aspect of any golfer's game. If you can't put a consistent swing on the ball, you'll never be able to be a consistent player. Having a swing you can easily replicate, even if it isn't the best or prettiest, is very important.

  The last thing I like about golf, and maybe the most important, is the time you get to spend out in nature with the people you're playing with, just spending time with them and building your relationships. I absolutely love getting out on the course with my girlfriend, friends, and even strangers. It really gives you a break from life, and lets you connect with those you're playing with. I've actually done a good deal of networking on the course when I got matched up with random players!
