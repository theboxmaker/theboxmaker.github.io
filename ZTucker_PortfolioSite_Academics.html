My First Few Tries

  I will be the first to admit that school has never been important to me, and as a result I never tried my best to get good grades or pay attention in class. It just didn't matter, everything would end up working itself out anyway. That's what I told myself for so long. I spent the longest time not doing anything to improve my chances of success in the future. I did okay in high school because I was a good test taker, but that didn't matter in college. I went right out of school to make my parents happy and did terribly. I skipped class all the time, didn't do any homework, and eventually decided to drop out after a year. That was my first attempt at college, and this is where the personal growth starts to happen.

  After my first failed attempt at college I just worked for the next year as much as I could, enjoying my life and doing whatever I wanted to. After that year ended, I tried to go back to school, this time of my own volition. It was also another way to keep my parents happy, but I did want to give it another shot. So I went back, this time for graphic design. I gave it an honest effort this time around, went to all of my classes, did my homework, the whole thing. The only problem now was that yes, I decided to go back, but I didn't change any of my bad habits. I was a major procrastinator and still didn't really care about what I was doing. I was just there because I knew I needed to get some education for my future. After another year there, I decided to take some time off again.

My Current Run

  During this break, I had goals. I wanted to do some deep self-reflection, look at my dreams and aspirations, and fix those bad habits that were holding me back in the past. My old mindset was that I needed motivation to get my work done. Throughout my reflection I discovered that while yes, motivation does help, it really boils down to discipline. Have a routine and stick to it. Make the sacrifices now so that you don't pay for it in the future. This realization was key in creating my new mindset, which is allowing me to come back on my current college attempt and do my best.

  Now, I'm feeling so good about college. I have an entirely new view coming into this and I believe that showed in my previous semester (Fall '24). Everything about that semester was better than previous attempts. My grades improved, my discipline, and my time management. And seeing the results from those changes in mindset have truly inspired me to keep going down this path. It also helps that I am actually interested in what I'm pursuing now.

  After I get my associate's degree I plan to enter the workforce and begin the next chapter in my life. My ideal career is to be a front end developer for a major company, or to have enough sidework to support myself and my family as a freelancer.
